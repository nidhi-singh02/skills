# Voice profile — <your name / handle>

A short description of how YOU write, so the skill can match your voice instead of sounding generic.
Fill this in (delete the prompts), save it as `~/.claude/voice-profile.md`, and the skill will read
it automatically. The more specific and honest this is, the better the output. If you don't have
one, the skill will ask you for 2-3 real posts and infer from those, but a written profile is better.

## TL;DR style card (one paragraph)
Write 2-4 sentences describing your voice as if briefing a ghostwriter. Casing (all-lowercase?
sentence case?), energy (dry / warm / hype), and the one or two things that make it unmistakably you.

## Tone
- Bullet the qualities: e.g. warm but not soft · self-deprecating · confident · community-first.
- Note what you are NOT (e.g. never corporate, never hype).

## Style mechanics
- Typical length (one-liners? short stacks? long threads?).
- Sentence shape (short/declarative? fragments OK?).
- Any punctuation tics (e.g. `->` arrows for lists, trailing "...", stretched vowels).
- How you format lists, questions, emphasis.

## Signature words & markers
- Words/phrases you actually use (e.g. "ship it", "tbh", "ngl", a vocative like "folks"/"chat").
- Your emoji palette (the specific few you use, and which one is "yours").
- Words/phrases you would NEVER use.

## Hook patterns (how you open)
- 2-4 opener styles that are yours (a confession, a bold claim, "here's how to…", a number).

## Closer / CTA patterns
- How you end (soft sign-off, a question, an accountability line). Any signature closer.

## Guardrails — do / don't
- **DO:** the fingerprints to keep even in short pieces.
- **DON'T:** hype words, clichés, or anything off-brand. (The skill also strips generic AI tells.)

## Learned (machine-appended)
The skill appends one dated line here from two sources, and reads them back before your next piece
so both kinds of lesson stick. Prune or edit freely; it's your file.
- Draft corrections (what you changed or rejected), e.g.
  `- 2026-07-05: rejected "game-changer"; prefers plain "useful"`.
- Post-launch retro findings (what the audience rewarded; see `references/retro.md`), e.g.
  `- 2026-07-05 · x · build/debug · time-saved hook · bookmarks 4x likes: keep the in-body save cue`.
